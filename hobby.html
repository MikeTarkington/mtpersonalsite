Hobby

I believe hobbies enrich our lives, can make us more talented, and help us develop many skills that are transferable to other aspects of life. In my lifetime, I have had many hobbies ranging from athletic to artistic activities. Having hobbies and partaking in activities of little seriousness or consequence can really help reduce stress and regenerate our good spirits so that we can be more productive in other areas of life with important consequences. A part of getting to know me is getting to know my recreational interests.

I was fortunate that, despite my humble beginnings, my parents were able to get me involved in a wide variety of athletic activities. As a child I had done gymnastics, swimming, and judo classes. Growing up I played just about every popular sport, whether on a school team, or just getting grouped up with friends and finding a field or court. For many of my teen years I was in love with doing skateboarding tricks. Apart from doing basic physical maintenance at the gym, most of my athletic hobbies died out as I reached adulthood. Basketball on weekends and some judo/jiu jitsu interests lasted into my adult years.

I've also had many non-athletic hobbies. When I was a teen, my father taught me how to shoot pool (8-ball) and that ("running a table") was something I've enjoyed well into adulthood. I played video games as a kid starting with Atari and still enjoy playing games on my PC today. As a kid, collecting comic books got me into art because I wanted to draw my favorite characters. My visual arts interests evolved over decades through a variety of urban art styles and painting. As a teen I played guitar (still do on rare occassion), and for many years I was a hip-hop/drum & bass vinyl DJ, collecting records on weekends, and performing at a few parties with friends.

At pretty much every point of my life I've had at least one hobby that was important to me. I expect to continue that trend for the rest of my life. Recently, learning to code on my own has been a hobby, and I aim to turn that interest into a career.
