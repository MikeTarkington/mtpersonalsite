Hobby

I believe hobbies enrich our lives, can make us more talented, and help us develop many skills that are transferable to other aspects of life. In my lifetime, I have had many hobbies ranging from athletic to artistic activities. Having hobbies and partaking in activities of little seroiusness or consequence can really help reduce stress and regenerate our good spirits so that we can be more productive in other areas of life with important consequences. A part of getting to know me is getting to know my recreational interests.

I was fortunate that, despite my humble beginings, my parents were able to get me involved in a wide variety of athletic activities. As a child I had done gymnastics, swimming, and judo classes. Growing up I played just about every popular sport, whether on a school team, or just getting grouped up with friends and finding a field or court. For many of my teen years I was in love with doing skateboarding tricks. Apart from doing basic physical maintenance at the gym, most of my athletic hobbies died out as I reached adulthood. Basketball on weekends and some judo/jiu jitsu interests lasted into my adult years.
